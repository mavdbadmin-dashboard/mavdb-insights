Josh Bartch: The Capital Markets Architect Behind MavDB Consulting

By [Publication Staff] | Capital Markets | Advisory

In the world of capital markets advisory, boutique firms live or die by the sharpness of their insight and the depth of their networks. Josh Bartch, Founder and CEO of MavDB Consulting LLC, has spent his career building both. From his base in San Juan, Puerto Rico, Bartch has quietly become one of the most sought-after advisors for international companies seeking to navigate the complexities of cross-border listings and Nasdaq uplisting — a specialty that demands not just regulatory knowledge, but a rare fluency in how different capital markets think, operate, and communicate with one another.

Building the Foundation

David Joshua Bartch's path to founding MavDB Consulting was shaped by years of direct exposure to the mechanics of public markets — how companies raise capital, how they present themselves to institutional and retail investors, and what separates the deals that close from the ones that don't. Long before MavDB existed as a firm, Bartch was developing the instincts that would define it: a relentless focus on execution, a preference for direct engagement over bureaucratic process, and a conviction that the best advisory relationships are built on genuine partnership rather than transactional service.

That philosophy crystallized in 2020 when Bartch established MavDB Consulting LLC. The timing was deliberate. Global capital markets were in flux — volatility had created both dislocation and opportunity, and international companies were increasingly looking toward U.S. exchanges, particularly Nasdaq, as a source of liquidity, visibility, and investor credibility. Bartch saw a gap: the bulge-bracket banks were largely indifferent to mid-market international companies, and the smaller advisory shops lacked the cross-border sophistication to do the work well. MavDB was designed to fill that gap precisely.

What Makes MavDB Different

From day one, MavDB Consulting was built around a single organizing principle: boutique rigor with global reach. The firm advises companies listed — or seeking listings — on six of the world's major exchanges, including Nasdaq, NYSE, the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX), the London Stock Exchange (LSE), Euronext, and the Canadian Securities Exchange (CSE). That breadth is not accidental. Bartch understood early that cross-border capital markets work requires fluency in multiple regulatory regimes, investor cultures, and market structures simultaneously.

"There's a reason most firms don't do this well," Bartch has said. "It requires you to hold two or three different regulatory frameworks in your head at the same time, understand what investors on each exchange actually want to see, and then build a bridge between them. That's the work."

The MavDB approach is hands-on and highly customized. Rather than deploying templated strategies, the firm works closely with each client to understand their specific capitalization structure, investor base, and business narrative before designing an advisory strategy. The result is counsel that is both technically precise and practically executable — a combination that has earned MavDB a reputation for getting deals done in markets where others struggle.

The Cross-Border Listings Specialty

If there is one area where Bartch has built particular authority, it is the complex world of cross-border listings. Companies domiciled in Australia, the United Kingdom, Canada, or Continental Europe face a significant challenge when they decide to pursue a U.S. listing: the rules are different, the investor expectations are different, and the communication standards are different. What works on the ASX does not always translate to Nasdaq, and the gap between those two realities has derailed more than a few promising companies.

Bartch has made closing that gap his professional mission. Through MavDB, he advises international issuers on everything from structural preparation and SEC disclosure requirements to investor relations strategy and timing. He has particular expertise in working with companies that have complex equity structures — convertible instruments, warrants, and other securities that require careful management as a company transitions between exchanges or seeks to broaden its U.S. investor base.

This expertise extends to his personal investment activity. Bartch has invested personally in multiple publicly traded companies alongside his advisory work, a practice that has deepened his understanding of how capital markets actually function from the investor's perspective — not just the issuer's. That dual lens, as both advisor and investor, gives MavDB counsel an unusual grounding in real-world market dynamics.

Why Puerto Rico

In recent years, Bartch relocated MavDB Consulting's operations to San Juan, Puerto Rico — a move that reflects both strategic calculation and genuine conviction. Puerto Rico's Act 60 tax incentive program has made the island an increasingly attractive base for financial services professionals and entrepreneurs, offering significant tax advantages for qualifying businesses and residents. But for Bartch, the move was about more than tax efficiency.

Puerto Rico occupies a unique geographic and cultural position: it is simultaneously a U.S. jurisdiction — with all the regulatory and banking infrastructure that entails — and a gateway to Latin America and the Caribbean. For a firm focused on cross-border capital markets work, that positioning is genuinely valuable. San Juan's growing community of finance professionals, founders, and investors has created an ecosystem that MavDB has been able to both benefit from and contribute to.

The island also offers what Bartch describes as an underappreciated quality of life advantage for the kind of intensive, relationship-driven work that capital markets advisory requires. The ability to build and maintain a global network while operating from a beautiful, accessible, U.S.-connected island has proven to be a differentiator in ways that extend beyond the financial.

Expanding the Mandate

Bartch's vision for MavDB Consulting has always been expansive. The firm continues to deepen its capabilities across its core service areas — uplisting advisory, cross-border listings, investor relations strategy, and capital formation — while also exploring adjacent opportunities at the intersection of capital markets and emerging technology.

Most notably, Bartch has co-founded NarrativeOS™ alongside strategic partner Scott Gelbard. The venture applies artificial intelligence to reputation management and narrative strategy — a natural extension of the work MavDB already does in helping companies communicate their story to investors and the broader market. NarrativeOS™ represents Bartch's conviction that the future of capital markets advisory will be shaped by the same forces reshaping every other industry: data, automation, and intelligent systems.

The Road Ahead

For Josh Bartch, the ambition is clear: to build MavDB Consulting into the definitive boutique advisory firm for international companies navigating U.S. capital markets. In a landscape where scale is often mistaken for capability, Bartch has made a different bet — that deep expertise, genuine relationships, and relentless execution will always win.

The evidence, so far, supports that thesis. Companies from six continents have sought MavDB's guidance on some of the most complex capital markets challenges they face. And from his perch in San Juan, Josh Bartch continues to do the work that matters: connecting companies with the capital they need to grow, and building the bridges that make global markets function.
